Overview

RoadSync is a logistics and payments software serving various customers in the trucking industry. B2B users include warehouse clerks, carriers, and tow and repair services, while individual truck drivers who make deliveries are the B2C users. Throughout my time at RoadSync, our main goal was to ensure a consistent and user-friendly experience across all our products, while also prioritizing the rapid development of new features.

I had the opportunity to make a significant impact by focusing on developing the native mobile app. The objective was to provide truck drivers with a tool to streamline their tasks and payments, ultimately establishing RoadSync as their preferred application while on the road.

User Signup Flow



Low Signup Rates

To make unloading trucks at delivery locations easier, RoadSync created a solution that allowed drivers to pay fees and invoice their employer for reimbursement directly from their cell phones. This eliminated the need for tedious paperwork and ensured that drivers provided all the required information, reducing delays between invoicing and reimbursement.

To do this, drivers could either create a RoadSync account or sign in as a guest. Creating an account had the benefit of avoiding the need to enter basic information for every invoice submission. Despite having hundreds of users, we noticed that the number of weekly signups remained in the single digits.


Improved Signup Flow

To tackle this issue, I undertook a redesign of the signup flow, incorporating user-friendly design, informative text, and illustrations. The goal was to enhance the usability of the app and provide a more engaging and intuitive experience for potential users.


Mobile App Concept



Overview

All of RoadSync's products were offered either for desktop-only or the responsive web, but we saw opportunity for its B2C driver app to be more useful to users as a native mobile app. I was responsible for the initial concept designs that we pitched to stakeholders for the mobile app.
