About RoadSync

RoadSync is a logistics & payments software with a variety of users, both B2B and B2C. Its B2B users consist of Warehouses, Carrier Services, and Tow & Repair while the B2C users are the truck drivers making deliveries. During my time at RoadSync, the company's goal was to expand its B2C user base and establish itself as the go-to application for these drivers on the road. As the first in-house designer, I got to make early product & visual design decisions. I also ideated and created the first round of designs for the mobile app.

New User Signup Flow

Low Driver Signup Rates

RoadSync's Driver App targeted B2C customers, mainly long-haul truck drivers. However, despite providing signup instructions, we only saw about 10 signups each week. To address this, the I redesign the signup flow with user-friendly design, informational text, and illustrations.

Before Redesign

HMW motivate drivers to sign up for a RoadSync Driver account?

  • Emphasize ease of account creation
  • Communicate immediate value to drivers by describing how it can solve their frustrations
  • Build credibility through good design

How might we make it easier for them to sign up?

  • Use recognizable UX patterns
  • Keep the form as short as possible at the beginning, giving them the option to add more information later.





Mobile Concepts

Spearheading RoadSync's Native App

I was responsible for spearheading the mobile app effort by creating concept designs. Our major question was, "why should we use a mobile app over a web app?" We decided to make a mobile app because:

  • Offline capability would allow truck drivers to create invoices in remote areas without internet and submit them later.
  • Push notifications offer a secure way to update users without sharing their personal information.
  • A mobile app centralizes schedules, invoices, and payments, providing users with a convenient and efficient hub.

Note: These concept designs weren't meant to be pixel-perfect, but to pitch to our focus groups and give stakeholders an idea of the product direction.







Product Discovery

What did RoadSync's users need?

We expanded our product offerings to truck drivers, leveraging the potential B2C users generated by our B2B users. However, drivers were hesitant to share information about themselves, making it difficult to understand their needs. Our challenge was to uncover insights about people who were reluctant to be known.




Making Sense of Insights from
User Research

After conducting preliminary research, we had more information than we knew what to do with. Affinity Maps and Empathy Maps came in handy for us to refer to as we drafted a few User Personas. From there, we could jump into Journey Mapping with real, human users in mind.
