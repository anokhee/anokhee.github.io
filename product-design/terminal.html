About Terminal

Terminal helps companies build out their remote teams by sourcing qualified engineers from around the world and serving as their Employer of Record. Terminal's B2B platform, TalentHub, helps customers hire and manage these remote engineers.

With the rise of remote work after the pandemic, there was opportunity for TalentHub to offer more to its users, who were hiring for companies like Calendly, Gusto, and Chime. I led design efforts to fulfill visions of the improved TalentHub, from early concepts to release.


Overview of What I Did

  • Led design from early concept ideation to launch for a major 0 → 1 product initiative
  • Rebranded & owned the design system for Terminal's B2B product
  • Designed concepts around a broad vision to get stakeholder buy-in
  • Collaborated with Product & Engineering to develop processes enabling us to ship new features, fast


The Problem

How can Terminal's TalentHub help companies hire the right engineering talent for their remote teams?


Although many candidates were sending applications in, none of them seemed like a good-enough fit for companies' hiring requirements.

Users knew Terminal had a databse of qualified engineering talent available, so why weren't they applying to their open roles?


Customers had a hard time differentiating one candidate from another.

With Terminal's Talent Acquisition team creating all the candidate profiles, each engineer in its database looked almost identical.


TalentHub felt like a collection of pages with no clear purpose tying them all together.

TalentHub had poor navigation, lacked a clear content hierarchy, and the user journey was unclear. The B2C Candidate Portal recently underwent major rebranding, making the inconsistency between the two products more apparent.



Final Designs

Talent Marketplace

Before the Talent Marketplace feature, customers could only review engineers after they applied and couldn't actively search for qualified talent. Now, customers can access a curated pool of talent to browse through, where they can take actions such as inviting, favoriting, or sharing an engineer's profile with their team. This empowers customers to proactively find the right talent.





TalentHub Dashboard

The TalentHub dashboard is a net-new feature. We noticed users had difficulty quickly identifying the most efficient path to achieve their hiring goals. The dashboard displays action items in order of priority, presented as a to-do list that users can reference at a glance. This design shows clear paths to complete tasks, and makes TalentHub feel less like a collection of random pages.





Job Creation Form

TalentHub's old job creation form was lengthy and unclear, so we recommended our users submit jobs through a Google form instead. However, that form was also rather lengthy and submitted details would disappear, so users couldn't track the status or review their submission without contacting us. The new, shorter form includes instructions for crafting effective job postings and lets users track and edit their submissions within the app itself.




Cost Calculator Concept

As a new feature, Terminal's Talent Marketplace risked being overlooked without context on its value. The Cost Calculator serves as an entry point and reference tool for customers to understand hiring costs and factors. It also suggests suitable candidates based on user input, encouraging them to explore the Talent Marketplace.






Design System & Rebranding

Standardizing Two Design Systems

TalentHub's MVP and Candidate Portal used different UI frameworks: Bootstrap and ChakraUI, respectively. To standardize branding and simplify future UI changes, I suggested a one-to-one swap from Bootstrap to ChakraUI for the MVP, leaving the UI and content unchanged. This approach streamlines the work of developers and ensures branding consistency across B2B and B2C products. Prioritizing this now also freed up my time to focus on rebranding and conceptual work concurrently.




Before the Visual Rebranding

As I mentioned before, TalentHub's branding needed a serious overhaul to match the Candidate Portal. I'm paraphrasing, but the general consensus around the vision for the branding was: "We want it to feel light, airy, and inviting." and "B2B doesn't have to be boring!"




Endless Explorations

The rebranding process proved challenging as it overlapped with concept work, causing it to fall behind schedule. Although I submitted a few branding options every week, the feedback I received remained the same: "It's difficult to make a decision with so few choices. The designer who rebranded the Candidate Portal recommended creating multiple design variations in the Figma file. This involved generating multiple iterations of the same screens to increase the chances of one standing out as the best option (or at least a starting point for further development).





Concepts

Conceptualizing on a Broad Vision

My Product Manager and I collaborated on creating concepts and continuously iterated on them. As we were still exploring product direction, the process felt experimental. Sometimes the product brief influenced the designs, and other times, the designs inspired the product briefs. Once stakeholders approved, we presented these concepts during an All-Hands meeting to generate enthusiasm.






(A Note About the Pink)

I used the concepts as an opportunity to push the color pink into Terminal’s branding because it's such a friendly and inviting color. This got some traction from stakeholders, but we received feedback that our concepts looked a bit off-brand when presented to the entire company. Because of this, we had to double-down at the rebranding stage to ensure that everyone was on the same page.


Sketching & Brainstorming

The product vision was broad, so there was room for experimentation. Crazy 8s sketching, sporadic notes, and stream-of-consciousness lived on my iPad and served as a reference for new ideas.
