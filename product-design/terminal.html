About Terminal

Terminal helps companies build out their remote teams by sourcing qualified engineers from around the world and serving as their Employer of Record. Terminal's B2B platform, TalentHub, helps customers hire and manage these remote engineers.

With the rise of remote work after the pandemic, there was opportunity for TalentHub to offer more to its users, who were hiring for companies like Calendly, Gusto, and Chime. I led design efforts to fulfill visions of the improved TalentHub, from early concepts to release.

The Problem

How can Terminal's TalentHub help companies hire the right engineering talent for their remote teams?


Although many candidates were sending applications in, none of them seemed like a good-enough fit for companies' hiring requirements.

Users knew Terminal had a databse of qualified engineering talent available, so why weren't they applying to their open roles?


Customers had a hard time differentiating one candidate from another.

With Terminal's Talent Acquisition team creating all the candidate profiles, each engineer in its database looked almost identical.


TalentHub felt like a collection of pages with no clear purpose tying them all together.

TalentHub had poor navigation, lacked a clear content hierarchy, and the user journey was unclear. The B2C Candidate Portal recently underwent major rebranding, making the inconsistency between the two products more apparent.


First 4 Weeks

Clarifying User Problems & setting up the design system

Collaborators: Product Manager, Front End Engineers, Talent Acquisition, Customer Success

Discovery Interviews

I learned a lot about the recruiting process by interviewing internal recruiters, whose role mirrored that of our users. Customer Support dealt firsthand with user issues in the app, so they quickly got me up to speed about existing pain points. These interviews played an important role in the discovery process before we had end users to interview.


Sketching & Brainstorming

The product vision was broad, so there was room for experimentation. Crazy 8s sketching, sporadic notes, and stream-of-consciousness lived on my iPad and served as a reference for new ideas.


Standardizing B2B & B2C Design Systems

TalentHub's MVP used Bootstrap while the Candidate Portal used ChakraUI. As a first step, I proposed a one-to-one swap to ChakraUI without changing any of the UI or content yet.

This would make it easier to standardize branding across B2B and B2C products, and for developers to make larger UI changes going forward. Prioritizing this work now also gave me the bandwidth to focus on concepts and rebranding work in parallel.



The Following 6 Weeks

Concept Designs & Rebranding

Collaborators: Product Manager, Greater Product/Design Teams, Business Development


Conceptualizing a Broad Vision

My Product Manager and I worked in tandem to create concepts, bouncing ideas off each other with each iteration. We hadn't narrowed down a product direction yet, so the process felt experimental at times. Sometimes product brief informed the designs and other times the designs inspired the product briefs. Once stakeholders gave us the green light, we used these concepts in an All-Hands presentation to get everyone excited.






A Note About the Pink

I used the concepts as an opportunity to push the color pink into Terminalâ€™s branding because it's such a friendly and inviting color. This got some traction from stakeholders, but we received feedback that our concepts looked a bit off-brand when presented to the entire company. Because of this, we had to double-down at the rebranding stage to ensure that everyone was on the same page.



Visual Rebranding

As I mentioned before, TalentHub's branding needed a serious overhaul to match the Candidate Portal. I'm paraphrasing, but the general consensus around the vision for the branding was: "We want it to feel light, airy, and inviting." and "B2B doesn't have to be boring!"




The Challenge

The team grew more frustrated with each week that went by. Because the rebranding was happening in parallel with the concept work, it started falling behind. Each week, I put together a few options, but I got the same feedback: "It's hard to say yes or no with so few options".




Endless Explorations

The designer who rebranded the Candidate Portal suggested that I keep creating explorations. This meant filling the Figma file with variations on the same screens. In doing it this way, the idea was that at least one of them was bound to stick out as "the one" (or, at least close enough to start getting somewhere).





Concluding Weeks

Final Designs

Collaborators: Product Manager, Greater Product/Design Teams, Business Development


Talent Marketplace

Customers could only review engineers after they applied and couldn't seek out qualified talent on their own. The Talent Marketplace enables customers to browse through a curated pool of qualified talent. From here, a user can invite an engineer to an interview, favorite them, or share their profile with their team.





Dashboard

The TalentHub dashboard was new. I proposed this as a solution to users being unable to discern the happy path at a glance. All the most important actions would be immediately visible, in priority order and in a to-do list format.





Job Creation Form

TalentHub's existing Job Creation form was long and confusing. So much so that we encouraged customers to use a Google form instead. While convenient, the form and all its details disappeared once a user hit "Submit". Users had no way to review what they'd submitted or checking on its status without reaching out to Terminal. The new Job Creation form is much shorter and provides instructions to help users write a good job posting. Users could track their job submission status in the app, and even review or edit their submissions.




Cost Calculator

After the launch, Terminal's Talent Marketplace would be a completely new feature. We feared that it would get overlooked without any context about what it is and how valuable it was. The Cost Calculator serves as both a reference tool and entry point into the Talent Marketplace. It helps customers understand the factors affecting the cost of hiring a remote engineer through Terminal. Based on input data from the users, the Cost Calculator suggests candidates in the Talent Marketplace who may be a good fit.
