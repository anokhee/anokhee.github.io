Overview

Terminal helps American companies build and manage their remote teams by sourcing global talent and serving as their Employer of Record. With the rise of post-pandemic remote work, Terminal expanded its B2C Candidate Portal, opening up new possibilities for companies hiring through Terminal.

As the designer for the Customer Products team, I identified growth opportunities and led the end-to-end design for Terminal's previously-fragmented B2B platform.

What are we solving?

We learned that companies of all sizes faced challenges in finding engineering talent with the desired qualifications. Large companies received numerous applications, but struggled to find candidates meeting their rigorous hiring requirements. Smaller companies, meanwhile, received few, if any, applications.

Terminal's existing B2B hiring platform, TalentHub, was basically a GUI built on top of a vendor applicant tracking system, and users were linked to external platforms to complete all the tasks needed to complete one single hire. This resulted in a disjointed user journey and time-consuming hiring process.


Process


Discovery

In order to gain a deep understanding of our users' needs, we employed various research methods. These methods included conducting interviews, analyzing customer success documentation, and performing competitive analysis. Through these comprehensive research efforts, we were able to identify the challenges faced by our users and uncover opportunities for growth.

Sitemapping

To begin, we conducted an evaluation of the existing platform. We used a sitemap to see how all the pages connected and decide which should be retained, enhanced, or discarded. The goal was to develop a refreshed product that would offer a smoother user experience by specifically addressing the major pain points discovered during our research.

User Journey Presentation

Next, the Product Manager and I collaborated to create a compelling user journey for TalentHub, centered around our persona â€“ a hiring manager named Holly. We carefully mapped out her experience, from the initial onboarding to her role in overseeing a remote team's progress six months later.

By developing a detailed user journey, we gained valuable insights into the specific challenges and needs she encountered throughout her journey, enabling us to design TalentHub in a way that effectively addressed their pain points.

We turned this user journey into a presentation for a company-wide All Hands to get everyone excited about the new-and-improved TalentHub.

Concept Designs

To supplement our user journey presentation and get the OK from stakeholders about our design direction, I created concept designs.

I explored a vibrant color palette to bring lightness and personality to TalentHub, aiming to break the mold of traditional B2B platforms. To differentiate TalentHub from its sister B2C platform, I incorporated a pink color into Terminal's branding. By thoughtfully selecting and integrating this new color, my goal was to create a visually distinctive and engaging experience for TalentHub users.





Color Palette Challenge

Although our concept designs got the "OK" from stakeholders, and we liked the ideas very much between the Customer Products team, we received some feedback saying that the experimental changes I had made to the color palette and general look-and-feel of TalentHub didn't fit Terminal's overall branding.

The next section will go more in-depth into how we implemented the new color palette and design system, but the image below shows the edits made to align the concepts more with Terminal's branding.


Design Iterations

I iterated on a talent marketplace candidate profile card, refining the design to showcase unique qualifications and attract recruiters. Incorporating user feedback and testing, I optimized the visual hierarchy, badge placement, and content presentation for an enhanced user experience. This iterative process led to a compelling profile card in the final MVP, increasing the chances of successful candidate-job matches.


Design System & Branding


Upgrades in Phases

Upgrading our MVP's components from Bootstrap to ChakraUI brought multiple benefits for future front-end changes. ChakraUI's streamlined and flexible framework simplified modifying and enhancing the user interface. With its extensive component library and focus on reusability, our team efficiently made adjustments and additions, ensuring a smoother and consistent user experience. This upgrade improved our responsiveness to evolving design requirements and boosted overall maintainability and scalability.


New Color Palette

The rebranding process faced delays as it overlapped with concept work. Despite submitting weekly branding options, the feedback consistently requested more choices. To address this, the designer proposed creating multiple design variations in the Figma file. This involved generating multiple iterations of the same screens to increase the chances of finding the best option or serving as a starting point for further development.

Design Updates

  1. Prominent Social Links

    When hiring managers are given only the option to "Accept" or "Reject" a candidate, they are limited in their ability to make nuanced decisions about hiring. Adding middle-ground options allows for a broader range of decision-making possibilities.
