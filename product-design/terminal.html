Timeframe

April - November 2022

Terminal helps companies build out their remote teams by sourcing qualified engineers from around the world and serving as their Employer of Record. Terminal's B2B platform, TalentHub, helps companies hire and manage these remote engineers.

With the rise of post-pandemic remote work, Terminal expanded its B2C database of engineering talent from Canada, Poland, and LatAm. This expansion provided an opportunity for Terminal to offer more to its B2B users: hiring managers and recruiters for tech companies based in the United States.

As the primary designer on the B2B products team, I identified opportunities for growth while leading end-to-end design efforts for the "new-and-improved" TalentHub.

Discover & Define

How can companies use TalentHub to find, hire, and manage the right engineering talent for their remote teams?


Empathizing

Understanding User Needs

Much of the initial UXR was completed before I joined Terminal, so the priority was to fast-track product design and release. However, as a new hire, I lacked some context about the current users and the competitive landscape, which are important consider when making user-centric design decisions. To quickly get the information I needed, I:

  • Interviewed internal team members, including recruiters who used TalentHub to recruit engineers for Terminal's B2C candidate portal.
  • Analyzed customer success documentation to understand our B2B users' existing pain points.
  • Performed competitive analysis to ensure that we would provide a competitive product and identify opportunities for differentiation.
  • Evaluated the existing product based on known usability heuristics to identify quick wins.

Identifying Growth Opportunities

"How Might We..."

From the pain points we uncovered during the research phase, we identified several opportunities for growth.


01

HMW help companies attract enough candidates meeting their hiring criteria?

Terminal had a large database of qualified engineering talent looking for work, but smaller companies weren't receiving many applications to their open roles.


02

HMW better showcase each candidate's unique skills, experiences, and specialties?

Candidate profiles looked like walls of text and did little to emphasize what may make each candidate uniquely qualified for the position they were applying to.


03

HMW implement UX that guide users towards successful talent acquisition?

Users struggled to identify the most efficient path to achieve their hiring goals due to confusing navigation, broken links, and poor content hierarchy.


04

HMW use compelling branding to set TalentHub apart from competitors?

The Talent Hub wasn't memorable compared to other recruiting dashboards, and the recent major re-branding of its sister product, the B2C Candidate Portal, highlighted the inconsistency between the two products.

Sitemapping

Sitemapping allowed us to visualize the structure and hierarchy of the existing TalentHub. We could see how various pages or features connected, giving us an idea of areas which could be improved with a new feature, updated UI, or microinteractions. During this stage, I took stock of problematic areas, as well as what was working well.



Defining Requirements

Once we had a rough idea of what our users needed, I worked with the PM to draft a Product Requirements Document.

  • Collaborative: Anyone on the team could view it and contribute feedback asynchronously.
  • Flexible: As we collected feedback, learned more about users and technical limitations, the PRD evolved in scope.
  • User-Centric: The PRD aligned the team on the importance of continuously advocating for users, ensuring that all decisions going forward are user-centered.
Presented Concepts

These are the concepts we presented to the entire company at kick-off. We approached the concept designs with the intention of generating many, many ideas before converging on the ones that seemed most promising. These concepts marked a significant departure from the previous version of TalentHub. Rather than serve one single purpose, we bundled several features, including a talent marketplace, dashboard, improved candidate hub, and job creation form into one cohesive platform.

A Note About the Pink

Early in the rebranding process, I suggested incorporating the color pink into the new color palette. Although this idea gained initial support from stakeholders, we received feedback that the concepts looked inconsistent with our overall brand when presented to the entire company. As a result, we had to focus even more on the rebranding stage to ensure that our design choices aligned with Terminal's brand identity.

Conclusion

The final designs.

01.

Talent Marketplace

Before the Talent Marketplace feature, customers could only review engineers after they applied and couldn't actively search for qualified talent. Now, customers can access a curated pool of talent to browse through, where they can take actions such as inviting, favoriting, or sharing an engineer's profile with their team. This empowers customers to proactively find the right talent.


02.

Candidate Profile

We revamped the candidate profile with improved information hierarchy and branding.



Candidate Profile Before



Candidate Profile After

03.

Revamped Dashboard

We noticed users had difficulty quickly identifying the most efficient path to achieve their hiring goals. The dashboard displays action items in order of priority, presented as a to-do list that users can reference at a glance. This design shows clear paths to complete tasks, and makes TalentHub feel less like a collection of random pages.



Dashboard Before



04.

Job Creation Form

TalentHub's old job creation form was lengthy and unclear, so we recommended our users submit jobs through a Google form instead. However, that form was also rather lengthy and submitted details would disappear, so users couldn't track the status or review their submission without contacting us. The new, shorter form includes instructions for crafting effective job postings and lets users track and edit their submissions within the app itself.


04.

Cost Calculator Concept

As a new feature, Terminal's Talent Marketplace risked being overlooked without context on its value. The Cost Calculator serves as an entry point and reference tool for customers to understand hiring costs and factors. It also suggests suitable candidates based on user input, encouraging them to explore the Talent Marketplace.
