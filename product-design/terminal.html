Overview

Terminal helps American companies build and manage their remote teams by sourcing global talent and serving as their Employer of Record. With the rise of post-pandemic remote work, Terminal expanded its B2C Candidate Portal, opening up new possibilities for companies hiring through Terminal.

As the designer for the Customer Products team, I identified growth opportunities and led the end-to-end design for Terminal's previously-fragmented B2B platform.

What are we solving?

We learned that companies of all sizes faced challenges in hiring engineering talent fitting their needs. Large companies received numerous applications, but struggled to find candidates meeting their rigorous hiring requirements. Smaller companies, meanwhile, received few, if any, applications.

Terminal's existing B2B hiring platform, TalentHub, did not serve any specific purpose yet; it was more like a brandless GUI built on top of a vendor applicant tracking system. Additionally, there was no streamlined channel of communication between users and their Talent Partners (Terminal's internal recruiting team) and users were linked to external platforms to complete all the tasks needed to complete one single hire. This resulted in a disjointed user journey and time-consuming hiring process.


Process


Discovery

In order to gain an even deeper understanding of our users' needs, we employed various research methods. These methods included conducting interviews, analyzing customer success documentation, and performing competitive analysis. Through this comprehensive research, we were able to identify the challenges faced by our users and uncover opportunities for growth and improved user satisfaction.

Sitemapping

Before putting pen to paper, I conducted an evaluation of the existing platform. I created a sitemap to understand how all the existing pages connected and decide what could be retained, enhanced, or discarded.


User Journey Presentation

Next, the Product Manager and I collaborated to create a compelling user journey presentation centered around our persona â€“ a hiring manager named Holly. We mapped her end-to-end experience with the new TalentHub, from the initial onboarding phase to her role in overseeing a remote team's progress six months later.

By presenting the user journey through the eyes of Holly, we aimed to demonstrate the positive impact and improvements that the TalentHub improvements would offer to users. The goal was to align the entire company around the shared vision and generate excitement for the upcoming changes.


Concept Designs

Once the stakeholders and the product team gave their approval, I completed the concept designs to go along with the user journey presentation. These designs aimed to make TalentHub look simple and modern, with fun elements and lively colors. The goal was to give the platform a light and interesting feel, different from the usual business-to-business style.





Color Palette Challenge

While the concept designs received approval from stakeholders and were well-received by the Customer Products team, we also received feedback stating that the experimental changes I made to the color palette and overall look-and-feel of TalentHub did not align with Terminal's branding.

Design System & Branding


Upgrades in Phases

Upgrading our MVP's components from Bootstrap to ChakraUI brought multiple benefits for future front-end changes. ChakraUI's streamlined and flexible framework simplified modifying and enhancing the user interface. With its extensive component library and focus on reusability, our team efficiently made adjustments and additions, ensuring a smoother and consistent user experience. This upgrade improved our responsiveness to evolving design requirements and boosted overall maintainability and scalability.


New Color Palette

The rebranding process faced delays as it overlapped with concept work. Despite submitting weekly branding options, the feedback consistently requested more choices. To address this, the designer proposed creating multiple design variations in the Figma file. This involved generating multiple iterations of the same screens to increase the chances of finding the best option or serving as a starting point for further development.


Talent Marketplace

About

I drew inspiration from user-friendly e-commerce platforms to enhance the candidate search process. By incorporating advanced search filters, candidate profiles, and integrated interview scheduling, TalentHub transformed into a streamlined and user-centric marketplace, saving time and improving the quality of recruitment outcomes.

Design Impacts

  1. Badges & Highlights

    I iterated on a talent marketplace candidate profile card, refining the design to showcase unique qualifications and attract recruiters. Incorporating user feedback and testing, I optimized the visual hierarchy, badge placement, and content presentation for an enhanced user experience. This iterative process led to a compelling profile card in the final MVP, increasing the chances of successful candidate-job matches.


  2. Personalization

    When hiring managers are limited to only "Accept" or "Reject" options for candidates, their ability to make nuanced decisions is restricted. By incorporating middle-ground options, a broader range of decision-making possibilities can be explored in hiring discussions.

  3. Favoriting Candidates

    When hiring managers are given only the option to "Accept" or "Reject" a candidate, they are limited in their ability to make nuanced decisions about hiring. Adding middle-ground options allows for a broader range of decision-making possibilities.
