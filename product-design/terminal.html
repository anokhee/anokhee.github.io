Overview

Designing TalentHub 2.0

Terminal helps companies build out their remote teams by sourcing qualified engineers from around the world and serving as their Employer of Record. Terminal's B2B platform, TalentHub, helps customers hire and manage these remote engineers.

With the rise of remote work after the pandemic, there was opportunity for TalentHub to offer more to its users, who were hiring for companies like Calendly, Gusto, and Chime. I led design efforts to fulfill visions of the improved TalentHub, from early concepts to release.



Problem Space

How can TalentHub help companies hire the right engineering talent for their remote teams?

Candidates were sending applications in, but it was hard for customers to find the right fit for their company. Users had no idea what it meant for Terminal to have a database "qualified" engineering talent applying to their open roles. Who are these people, and why has Terminal deemed them "qualified"?

TalentHub felt like a collection of pages with no clear purpose tying them all together. TalentHub used an off-brand color palette, lacked a clear content hierarchy, and the user journey was unclear. The B2C Candidate Portal recently underwent major rebranding, making the inconsistency between the two products more apparent.

Interviewing & Brainstorming

During the early days of my role at Terminal, I had little context on the day-to-day of a recruiter. So, to get clarity around problems with TalentHub, I interviewed several internal Terminal members.

Talent Acquisition provided important insight because their role mirrored that of our users. I learned a lot about the recruiting process and potential pain points faced by all recruiters. Customer Support dealt with user issues in the existing app. These interviews played important role in the discovery process during a time where we didn't have many end users to interview yet.



Standardizing B2B & B2C Design Systems

TalentHub was built using Bootstrap, a frontend library with components notoriously painful to customize.

I proposed a one-to-one swap from Bootstrap to ChakraUI (used by the Candidate Portal) without changing any UI or content yet. This would make it easier to standardize branding across B2B and B2C products, and for developers to make larger UI changes going forward. The devs were happy to oblige and prioritizing this work now also gave me the bandwidth to focus on concepts and rebranding work in parallel.

Ideating on a Broad Vision

While creating concepts, my Product Manager and I worked in tandem, both of us bouncing ideas off each other with each iteration. We hadn't narrowed in on a product direction yet, so the process felt experimental at times. Sometimes product brief informed the designs and other times the designs inspired the product briefs. Once we got the green light, we used these concepts in a company-wide presentation to get everyone excited about the future of TalentHub.


A Note About the Pink

The concept designs were completed before the rebranding. I used them as an opportunity to weasel the color pink into Terminal’s branding. I was so hellbent on pink at the time was because it's such a friendly and inviting color. This got some traction initially, but we received feedback that our concepts looked a bit off-brand when presented. We had to double-down at the rebranding stage to ensure that everyone was on the same page. The pink agenda turned out to be a dud.



Creating a new brand identity for TalentHub



Visual Rebranding

As I mentioned before, TalentHub's branding needed a serious overhaul to match the Candidate Portal. I'm paraphrasing, but the general consensus around the vision for the branding was: "We want it to feel light, airy, and inviting." and "B2B doesn't have to be boring!"




Endless Explorations

Because the rebranding was happening in parallel with the concept work, it started falling behind. Each week, I put together a few options, but I got the same feedback: "It's hard to say yes or no with so few options".

The team grew more frustrated with each week that went by. The designer who rebranded the Candidate Portal suggested that I keep creating explorations. This meant filling the Figma file with variations on the same screens. In doing it this way, the idea was that at least one of them was bound to stick out as "the one" (or, at least close enough to start getting somewhere).





Final Designs






Job Creation Form

TalentHub's existing Job Creation form was long and confusing. So much so that we encouraged customers to use a Google form instead. While convenient, the form and all its details disappeared once a user hit "Submit". Users had no way to review what they'd submitted or checking on its status without reaching out to Terminal. The new Job Creation form is much shorter and provides instructions to help users write a good job posting. Users could track their job submission status in the app, and even review or edit their submissions.




Cost Calculator

After the launch, Terminal's Talent Marketplace would be a completely new feature. We feared that it would get overlooked without any context about what it is and how valuable it was. The Cost Calculator serves as both a reference tool and entry point into the Talent Marketplace. It helps customers understand the factors affecting the cost of hiring a remote engineer through Terminal. Based on input data from the users, the Cost Calculator suggests candidates in the Talent Marketplace who may be a good fit.
