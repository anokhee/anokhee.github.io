Overview

Terminal helps American companies build and manage their remote teams by sourcing global talent and serving as their Employer of Record. With the rise of post-pandemic remote work, Terminal expanded its B2C Candidate Portal, opening up new possibilities for companies hiring through Terminal.

As the designer for the Customer Products team, I transformed Terminal's previously-fragmented B2B product into a fully-fledged hiring platform.

What are we solving?

Companies of all sizes faced challenges in hiring engineering talent fitting their needs. Large companies received great interest from many qualified applicants, but struggled to find candidates meeting their rigorous hiring requirements. Meanwhile, smaller companies struggled to find candidates who could fulfill their minimum requirements.

Terminal's existing B2B platform, TalentHub, didn't serve any specific purpose yet; it was basically a brandless GUI built on top of a vendor applicant tracking system. The goal was ambiguous: to revamp TalentHub into a platform offering enough value to acquire and retain B2B customers.


Process


Discovery

During discovery, we set out to understand why users may have been struggling to hire the talent they needed.

To do this, we employed various research methods, including conducting interviews, analyzing customer success documentation, and performing competitive analysis. Through this comprehensive research, we were able to identify the challenges faced by our users and uncover opportunities for growth and improved user satisfaction.


Pain Points

  1. Long Wait Times
    Users had to wait for candidates to apply to them, or for their Talent Partner on Terminal's internal recruiting team to provide recommendations. This resulted in extended times-to-fill for companies as they relied on their Talent Partners to complete tasks sequentially.

  2. Lack of Streamlined Communication
    Users had no efficient way to communicate with their Talent Partners and were directed to external platforms like Slack or Google Forms to complete various tasks necessary for finalizing a single hire. This led to a fragmented user experience and exacerbated the frustrations of a time-consuming hiring process.

  3. Inadequate Candidate Profiles
    Candidate profiles lacked emphasis on each candidate's unique qualities. Users were forced to use external platforms like Slack or Google Forms to complete tasks required for hiring, further complicating the user journey and adding to the frustrations of the hiring process.

  4. Credibility Concerns
    Users had doubts about the skill level and credibility of Terminal's candidate pool.

  5. Lack of Brand Identity
    The Talent Hub lacked memorability compared to other recruiting dashboards, and the recent rebranding of its sister product, the B2C Candidate Portal, highlighted the inconsistency between the two products.

User Journey Presentation

Next, the Product Manager and I collaborated to create a compelling user journey presentation centered around our persona – a hiring manager named Holly. We mapped her end-to-end experience with the new TalentHub, from the initial onboarding phase to her role in overseeing a remote team's progress six months later.

By presenting the user journey through the eyes of Holly, we aimed to demonstrate the positive impact and improvements that the TalentHub improvements would offer to users. The goal was to align the entire company around the shared vision and generate excitement for the upcoming changes.


Concept Designs

Once the stakeholders and the product team gave their approval, I completed the concept designs to go along with the user journey presentation. These designs aimed to make TalentHub look simple and modern, with fun elements and lively colors. The goal was to give the platform a light and interesting feel that differentiates it from traditional B2B software.





New Color Palette

While the concept designs received approval from stakeholders and were well-received by the Customer Products team, we also received feedback stating that the experimental changes I made to the color palette and overall look-and-feel of TalentHub did not align with Terminal's branding.

This required several rounds of explorations on various themes before landing on the one that product, engineering, and marketing could agree upon.


Visual Redesign in Phases


Talent Marketplace

About

I drew inspiration from user-friendly e-commerce platforms to enhance the candidate search process. By incorporating advanced search filters, candidate profiles, and integrated interview scheduling, TalentHub transformed into a streamlined and user-centric marketplace, saving time and improving the quality of recruitment outcomes.

Design Impacts

  1. Badges & Highlights

    I iterated on a talent marketplace candidate profile card, refining the design to showcase unique qualifications and attract recruiters. Incorporating user feedback and testing, I optimized the visual hierarchy, badge placement, and content presentation for an enhanced user experience. This iterative process led to a compelling profile card in the final MVP, increasing the chances of successful candidate-job matches.


  2. Personalization

    When hiring managers are limited to only "Accept" or "Reject" options for candidates, their ability to make nuanced decisions is restricted. By incorporating middle-ground options, a broader range of decision-making possibilities can be explored in hiring discussions.

  3. Favoriting Candidates

    When hiring managers are given only the option to "Accept" or "Reject" a candidate, they are limited in their ability to make nuanced decisions about hiring. Adding middle-ground options allows for a broader range of decision-making possibilities.
