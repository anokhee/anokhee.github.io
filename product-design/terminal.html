Overview

Terminal helps American companies build and manage their remote teams by sourcing global talent and serving as their Employer of Record. With the rise of post-pandemic remote work, Terminal expanded its B2C Candidate Portal, opening up new possibilities for companies hiring through Terminal.

As the designer for the Customer Products team, I identified growth opportunities and led the end-to-end design for Terminal's previously-fragmented B2B platform.

What are we solving?

Companies of all sizes faced challenges in finding engineering talent with the desired qualifications, although the reasons for these difficulties varied.

Large companies, for instance, received numerous applications but struggled to find candidates who met their hiring requirements. On the other hand, smaller companies received very few, if any, applications at all. Furthermore, Terminal's existing B2B platform consisted of multiple disconnected user interfaces that were built on a vendor applicant tracking system. This led to a disjointed hiring process, which required multiple external platforms (Google Forms, Slack, and others) to complete a single hire.

To address the need for qualified engineering talent, our main objective was to redesign the B2B platform. The redesign aimed to streamline the hiring experience on the hiring managers' end.


Process


Discovery

In order to gain a deep understanding of our users' needs, we employed various research methods, including conducting interviews with both customers and our internal team, analyzing customer success documentation, and performing competitive analysis. Through these comprehensive research efforts, we were able to identify the challenges faced by our users and uncover opportunities for growth and differentiation in the recruiting software market.

Sitemapping

To tackle the insights gained during our discovery phase, we conducted an evaluation of the existing platform. Our aim was to identify the features that should be retained, enhanced, or discarded. By doing so, we intended to develop a refreshed product that would offer a smoother user experience, specifically addressing the major pain points discovered during our research.

Journey Mapping

During the research and ideation phase, the Product Manager and I collaborated to create a compelling user journey for TalentHub, centered around our persona â€“ a hiring manager named Holly. We carefully mapped out her experience, from the initial onboarding to her role in overseeing a remote team's progress six months later.

By developing a detailed user journey for Holly, we gained valuable insights into the specific challenges and needs she encountered throughout her journey. This deep understanding of users like Holly allowed us to design TalentHub in a way that effectively addressed their pain points. Furthermore, by focusing on a single persona, we achieved a more cohesive and user-centric product that strongly resonated with our target audience.

Concept Designs

To supplement our user journey presentation, I created concept designs, bundling several new features into a cohesive platform, resulting in a seamless hiring process.

I explored a vibrant color palette to bring lightness and personality to TalentHub, aiming to break the mold of traditional B2B platforms. To differentiate TalentHub from its sister B2C platform, I incorporated a fresh color into Terminal's branding. By thoughtfully selecting and integrating this new color, my goal was to create a visually distinctive and engaging experience for TalentHub users.

Design System & Branding


Upgrades in Phases

Upgrading our MVP's components from Bootstrap to ChakraUI brought multiple benefits for future front-end changes. ChakraUI's streamlined and flexible framework simplified modifying and enhancing the user interface. With its extensive component library and focus on reusability, our team efficiently made adjustments and additions, ensuring a smoother and consistent user experience. This upgrade improved our responsiveness to evolving design requirements and boosted overall maintainability and scalability.


New Color Palette

The rebranding process faced delays as it overlapped with concept work. Despite submitting weekly branding options, the feedback consistently requested more choices. To address this, the designer proposed creating multiple design variations in the Figma file. This involved generating multiple iterations of the same screens to increase the chances of finding the best option or serving as a starting point for further development.



Candidate Profile

Given more time, I would have aligned the design more closely with the concept, which aimed to acknowledge the intricate nature of evaluating job candidates. Nevertheless, these improvements were implemented for the initial release.
