Designing Swiftly in a Controversial Domain

Gracie excelled at engagement but couldn't yet guide prospective candidates through the full application experience. This left recruiters chasing candidates down to collect resumes and other pre-screening information, which was inefficient for both sides.

Solving this on the design side required contending with a number of challenges.

2. Preventing User Alienation

Grayscale prioritized rapid shipping, so my capacity to conduct formal research was limited. But, I knew chatbot-driven hiring risked alienating jobseekers if designed poorly. To take extra care on the design side, I intentionally sought out the most critical perspectives.

Jobseekers' mistrust of AI hiring processes was usually rooted in fear of AI bias gatekeeping certain applications from ever reaching a human recruiter.

3. The Business Case for Accessibility

The truth was that Grayscale took real care to handle candidate information securely. My focus was to make that commitment visible to jobseekers by advocating internally for edge cases where applicants risked exclusion and designing UI patterns that reassured jobseekers that every application would be considered fairly.

The business case for accessible, secure chatbot designs is that in high-volume hiring, customers need to keep their pipelines full. Making applications clear and accessible to as many candidates as possible directly serves that interest.

Solution

I designed conversational flows, mobile-first UI patterns, and plain-language microcopy that reduced friction while reinforcing trust. These interventions covered everything from document upload to demographic disclosure. A good example of this work is the redesigned EEOC survey experience, which worked consistently across SMS, WhatsApp, and web.

Impact

My design allowed candidates to complete EEOC surveys securely across all channels, while preserving conversational flow and strengthening trust in Gracie. This work helped Grayscale to maintain compliance at a massive scale, and contributed to improved completion rates.
