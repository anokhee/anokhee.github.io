Visual Synthesizer

Hardware Prototyping, Computer Graphics



Overview

Within a 2-week period and without prior experience with robotics, I challenged myself to design and develop a Visual Synthesizer. The goal was to explore the potential for playfulness in an arguably logic-oriented world of computing and robotics.


Background

As I explore the possibilities of coding as a creative medium, I find myself coming back to the things that brought me joy during my childhood.

The hardware aspect of this project draws from the function and aesthetic of old MIDI synthesizers while the software is inspired by video game character generators.

Interactive Vector-Based Graphics


Like a MIDI synthesizer does with sound samples, the Visual Synthesizer allows users to twist knobs and push buttons to adjust the geometric proportions of the default face pictured in Fig 03.

The result is a virtually-endless array of unique faces that, by taking advantage of the scalability of vector graphics, allows the user flexibility similar to that of Nintendo's Miis or Apple's Memojis.
