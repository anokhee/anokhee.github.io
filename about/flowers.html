Botanicals (2020)




Overview

Generating endless nonexistent flower varieties with code.

For this project, my goal was to generate 2D fictional flowering plants with Lindenmayer Systems. Although this project is ongoing, it produces some interesting output in its current state. Feel free to check out the demo or continue reading to learn more about the process.


Background & Moodboard

The unusual onset of Spring 2020.

After a gloomy Atlanta winter followed by the loneliness of social distancing in March, the bright foliage and blooms accompanying the onset of Spring 2020 felt especially interesting and beautiful.


Over the course of a few walks around my neighborhood, I collected several leaves and flowers with the intention of pressing them inside a book. But, they looked so pretty that I knew I had to replicate how I felt about them with code.


Process & Progress

Iteration #1: Fractals - the patterns of chaos and plant-like structures.

Like many roller coaster rides and great romances, this project began with a line...and then another line, and then another line, and then another...until they formed a basic fractal tree (which is where the metaphor ends).

Although these trees look pretty enough on their own, I wanted to explore a more natural and flowery-looking approach.

Iteration #2: Botanical grammar with L-Systems.

In the second iteration, I was able to achieve more natural-looking flower structures by implementing L-Systems to generate self-similar fractals.

In this case, the L-System begins with a character string (called an axiom) and continuously, recursively applies a rule to the original axiom with some randomization. The results appear leafier and daintier than the basic fractals in Iteration #1.

Iteration #3: Rudimentary flowers.

By adding a unique symbol to the end of an L-Systems rule, I was able to write configurations for a flower to appear at certain endpoints of the stem.



Sketching the way into a JavaScript object.

Flower editor mini web application.


After experimenting with different options, I decided that the best way to create a wide variety of flower shapes was to draw a continuous Bezier curve looped x number of times around a central point. I wrote a separate flower editor with to make it easier to experiment with proportions.
