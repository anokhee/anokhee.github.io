iLearn Landing Page



Context

About iLearn and my role.

iLearn is a mathematics e-learning solution for K-12, college, and at-home use, with the purpose of filling gaps in students’ knowledge of common core K-12 math.

My primary duties involved testing the product for quality assurance, but I was able to explore my design interests by contributing to marketing efforts in the updated landing page.


The impetus for a new landing page...

Analytics reports reflected that most visitors to iLearn’s site spent an average of only a few seconds on the homepage before clicking away. We realized that this could mean a few things:

  • 1) iLearn’s homepage was not optimized for SEO - iLearn was appearing in search results for products unrelated to math education and accidentally attracting clicks from users from the wrong target demographic.
  • Users couldn’t find what they were looking for - The current homepage was not accurately or succinctly conveying information about iLearn’s services.

The new design featured updated copy and a cutesy look that complied with the overall look and feel of iLearn's application. I even drew some of the illustrations!

Low-Fidelity Mockups

Updated copy that highlights only the important things.

During the wireframing and prototyping process, my supervisor and I tried to develop an path of information that made sense. iLearn’s main users are K-12 and college math instructors, school administrators, and parents searching for an at-home solution for their children’s math educational needs. When writing the new copy, we tried to answer FAQs in decreasing order of importance.
