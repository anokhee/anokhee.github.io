iLearn Marketing Site

About iLearn & my role.

iLearn is a mathematics e-learning solution for K-12, college, and in-home use. Its purpose is to fill gaps in students’ knowledge of common core K-12 math. My primary duties at iLearn involved testing the product for quality assurance, but I was able to explore my design interests by contributing to the updated landing page.

The impetus for a new landing page.

Analytics reports reflected that many visitors to iLearn’s site spent an average of only a few seconds on the homepage before clicking away. We realized that this could mean a few things:

  • iLearn’s homepage was not optimized for SEO. iLearn was appearing in search results for products unrelated to math education. It may have been attracting clicks from users outside the intended target demographic. For example, college students in very advanced math courses or those interested in hobby mathematics.
  • Users couldn’t find what they were looking for. The current homepage was not accurately or succinctly conveying information about iLearn’s services.
  • Outdated UI negatively affecting the brand.

The revamped design featured updated, SEO-friendly copy and a cutesy look that aligned with the overall look and feel of iLearn's application. I even drew some of the illustrations!

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Updated copy highlighting the important things.

During the wireframing and prototyping process, my supervisor and I tried to develop a path of information based on relevance to users. iLearn’s main users are K-12 and college math instructors, school administrators, and parents searching for an at-home solution for their children’s math educational needs. When writing the new copy, we tried to answer FAQs in descending order of importance.
