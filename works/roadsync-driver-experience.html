Overview

RoadSync has a variety of users, both B2B and B2C. Its B2B users consist of Warehouses, Carrier Services, and Tow & Repair while its B2C users are the truck drivers that work with them.

My Design Process

Trucking Invoices

The Problem

Once a truck driver arrives at their destination, a third party (e.g. warehouse worker) comes out to the dock to unload the contents of the truck. This can save the driver the time and energy it takes to unload their truck themselves, but usually comes at a fee called a lumper fee.

Lumper fees can be anywhere from $50 to $400 right out of the truck driver's pocket. The carrier company is responsible for reimbursing this payment -- which can take up to 2-4 months.

Evaluating Research

To figure out what drivers wanted out of their digital experience, we needed to learn more about them as people. Unfortunately, we ran into one big problem: it was difficult to find truck drivers interested in participating in user interviews. Luckily, the ubiquity of social media gave us many other avenues of exploration into their lives and minds.

Personas

After compiling all our research, we developed personas reflecting RoadSync's major user bases: truck drivers, carriers, brokers, and tow and repair services. For the purpose of this case study, the truck driver and Owner-Operator were the most relevant personas as they'd be the only ones using RoadSync's B2C features.
