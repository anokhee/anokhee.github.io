A Tiny Flower Gallery

Background & Moodboard

The unusual onset of Spring 2020.

In Atlanta, a gloomy winter preceded the start of a long period of loneliness and anxiety of social distancing. By the time spring arrived, the bright foliage and accompanying blooms felt especially interesting and beautiful.

During walks around my neighborhood, I collected leaves and flowers to press and hang up on the walls in my apartment. I felt so inspired by them, that I just had to replicate them with code.

Process

Basic Fractal Trees

These fractals look cute and plantlike, but they resemble trees more than they resemble flowers. While I liked the results of this method, I wanted to explore a more natural and flowery-looking approach.

L-Systems, or fancy pants grammar for fancy plants.

L-Systems are a type of computational grammar used to generate complex, self-similar fractals. They can be used to model natural phenomena, such as plant growth and animal morphology.

An L-System begins with a character string (called an axiom). Until the defined exit condition is met, the ruleset is continuously, recursively applied to the original axiom. The result is a complex character string serving as an instruction set:

What kind of flower is that?

By adding a unique symbol to the end of an L-Systems rule, I was able to write configurations for a flower to appear at certain endpoints of the stem.
