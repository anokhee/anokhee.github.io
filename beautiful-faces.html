Beautiful Faces

Computer Graphics, Graphic Design

View Live Demo
Overview

For many, learning how to use tools like Adobe Illustrator or Inkscape can feel like an uphill battle.

I developed Beautiful Faces to explore ways to exploit the programmable nature of SVG. This web-friendly program allows users to draft and export custom vector graphics to SVG or PNG from the comfort of their web browser without touching the pen tool.


Drafting Vectors

Using sliders, users can tweak the colors and geometric proportions of a default face to their liking.

The face is drawn to the screen as a collection of Bezier curves and ellipses.

Creations can be exported as an SVG or PNG file.

If the image is saved as an .svg and opened in a vector graphics editor like Illustrator or Inkscape, all paths will be retained and fully editable.


Exploring Other Use Cases

Exploring further use cases in user experience.

Trustworthy Interactions Between Users

In community-based sites and applications, users are encouraged to upload a profile picture. Unfortunately, those who choose to use the provided default profile image can appear casual/non-participatory at best and creepy at worst.

Immemorable at best, creepy at worst

The grey, default Slack photo is not memorable and slightly ominous. Without a photo, other members of this Slack channel are likely to overlook or distrust this message if they are unfamiliar with the sender.

Impression of involvement

Although this picture is a cartoon, other members of the Slack channel are more likely to remember seeing me engage in discussions and less likely to distrust my message.

Beautiful Faces can provide a quick solution for users who don't feel like uploading a photo of themselves to a site, but want to feel like a regular member of the community.

Inclusive default identicons
Most default profile images feature decidedly male silhouettes with caucasian-looking hairstyles and features.

While this may seem harmless enough, it reinforces the idea of the white male user as the default person, which can inadvertently alienate minority users.


With a few lines of code, Beautiful Faces can be configured to generate thousands of on-brand images.

The result can include faces that are more gender-neutral, inclusive, and lighthearted than the typical default profile picture.

As a bonus, SVG is highly performant and scalable, which has benefits from a software engineering perspective on the front end and back end.
