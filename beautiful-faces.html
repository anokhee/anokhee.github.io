Beautiful Faces

Computer Graphics, Graphic Design



Overview

For many, learning how to use tools like Adobe Illustrator or Inkscape can feel like an uphill battle.

Beautiful Faces exploits the programmable nature of SVG, allowing users to draft and export custom vector graphics to SVG or PNG format from the comfort of their web browser.


How it Works

Using sliders, users can tweak the colors and geometric proportions of a face to their liking.

Using P5.js, a basic face is drawn to the browser. The face is comprised of several ellipses and Bezier curves plotted along dynamic vertex points on the computational plane.


User creations can be exported as an SVG or PNG file.

If the image is saved as an .svg and opened in a vector graphics editor like Illustrator or Inkscape, all paths will be retained and fully editable.

Exploring Use Cases

In what other ways can this software be useful?

Aside from drafting vector graphics, Beautiful Faces can be used as a solution to UX-related issues. Below are two possible use cases:



1) Custom Profile Pictures

In community-based sites and applications, users are encouraged to upload a profile picture. Unfortunately, those who choose to stick with the provided default profile image can appear casual/non-participatory at best and creepy at worst.

Beautiful Faces can provide a quick solution for users who don't feel like uploading a photo of themselves to a site, but want to feel like a regular member of the community.



2) Creative Default Identicons

Most social media sites' default identicons feature decidedly male silhouettes with caucasian-looking hairstyles and features. While this may seem harmless enough, it may reinforce the idea of white men as the default, inadvertently alienating minority users.

With a few lines of code, Beautiful Faces can be configured to generate thousands of on-brand images that are more gender-neutral, inclusive, and lighthearted than the typical default profile picture.

As a bonus, SVG is highly performant and scalable, which has benefits from a software engineering perspective on the front end and back end.
