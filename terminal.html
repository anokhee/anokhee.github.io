Overview

Helping companies hire the right engineering talent for their remote teams.

Terminal helps companies build out their remote engineering teams. On the B2C side, Terminal sources qualified engineers from Canada, LatAm, and Europe and serves as their Employer of Record. On the B2B side is TalentHub, a talent platform which helps customers hire and manage these remote engineers. TalentHub users are people hiring for companies like Nextdoor, Gusto, and Chime.

When I started at Terminal, TalentHub was a barebones MVP, and felt like a collection of pages with no clear purpose tying them all together. With remote hiring on the rise after the pandemic, Terminal saw opportunity for TalentHub to offer more to its users. I led design efforts to fulfill visions of TalentHub 2.0, from early concepts to its big release. This project was a significant cross-departmental effort, kicking off 4 months before its launch.


Design System Setup

Standardizing styles across B2B & B2C products.

Collaborators: Front End Engineers & Designers

The Challenge

At the beginning, TalentHub used an off-brand color palette, lacked a clear content hierarchy, and the user journey was unclear. On top of that, its sister product, the B2C Candidate Portal, had recently undergone a major rebranding, making it even more apparent that there was little consistency across the two products.


Bootstrap to ChakraUI

One of the reasons TalentHub felt so disjointed from the Candidate Portal was because it was built using Bootstrap. For those unfamiliar with Bootstrap, it is easy to spot and its components are notoriously painful to customize.

As a first step, I proposed a one-to-one swap from Bootstrap to ChakraUI without changing any UI or content yet. This would make it easier to standardize branding across B2B and B2C products, and for developers to make larger UI changes going forward. The devs were happy to oblige and prioritizing this work now also gave me the bandwidth to focus on concepts and rebranding work in parallel.




Ideation Process

Sketching, brainstorming, and interviewing.

Collaborators: Product Manager, Talent Acquisition, Customer Success

To supplement research, I interviewed internal Terminal members. Talent Acquisition provided important context because their role mirrored that of our users. I learned a lot about the recruiting process and potential pain points faced by all recruiters. Customer Support dealt with user issues in the existing app. The information gleaned from these interviews played an important role in the discovery process during a time where we didn't have many users to interview yet. Some of the concepts in the next session wouldn't have existed at all without them.



Concept Designs

Getting stakeholder buy-in & generating hype around
the future of TalentHub.

Collaborators: Product Manager, Greater Product/Design Teams, Business Development

Ideating on a Broad Vision

With a timeline of less than 4 months, I needed to create several concepts for the potential future of TalentHub, and fast. The Product Manager on my team and I worked in tandem, both of us bouncing ideas off each other with each iteration. We hadn't narrowed in on a product direction yet, so the process felt experimental at times. Sometimes product brief informed the designs and other times the designs inspired the product briefs.

After we were given the green light, we used these same concepts in a company-wide presentation to get everyone excited about the future of TalentHub.


Why It's Pink

The concept designs were completed before the rebranding. I used them as an opportunity to weasel the color pink into Terminal’s branding. I was so hellbent on pink at the time was because it's such a friendly and inviting color. This got some traction initially, but we received feedback that our concepts looked a bit off-brand when presented. We had to double-down at the rebranding stage to ensure that everyone was on the same page. The pink agenda turned out to be a dud.







Rebranding

Creating a brand identity for TalentHub.

As I mentioned before, TalentHub's branding needed a serious overhaul to match the Candidate Portal. I'm paraphrasing, but the general consensus around the vision for the branding was: "We want it to feel light, airy, and inviting. Also, B2B doesn't have to be boring."


The Challenge

Because the rebranding was happening in parallel with so many other things, it started to fall behind. Each week, I put together about 3-4 color palettes for the larger Product Team, hoping to get their approval. But I kept getting the same feedback. "It's hard to say yes or no with so few options," they said. The team grew more frustrated with each week that went by without considerable progress.


Endless Explorations

The designer who rebranded the Candidate Portal suggested that I keep creating explorations. This meant filling the Figma file with variations on the same screens. In doing it this way, the idea was that at least one of them was bound to stick out as "the one" (or, at least close enough to start getting somewhere).

Final Designs

Talent Marketplace

About this Feature

Terminal's Customers could only review engineers after they applied. They had no way of using Terminal to seek out qualified talent on their own. The Talent Marketplace enables customers to browse through a curated pool of qualified talent. From here, a user can invite an engineer to an interview, favorite them, or share their profile with their team.



Dashboard

About this Feature

TalentHub's existing Job Creation form was long and confusing. So much so that we encouraged customers to use a Google form instead. While convenient, the form and all its details disappeared once a user hit "Submit". Users had no way to review what they'd submitted or checking on its status without reaching out to Terminal. The new Job Creation form is much shorter and provides instructions to help users write a good job posting. Users could track their job submission status in the app, and even review or edit their submissions.



Candidate Profile

About this Feature

The existing Candidate Profiles needed some improvement. They didn't provide users with a clear picture of who each candidate is as a human being, or paint a credible picture of their quality. For users, it was difficult to know where the candidate was in the hiring process so they could take action. Additionally, these profiles lacked a seamless user experience. The new Candidate Profile provides users with a resume-like profile of each candidate. Salary expectations, start date, and remote readiness is clearly displayed with veritication.

Cost Calculator

About this Feature

After the launch, Terminal's Talent Marketplace would be a completely new feature. We feared that it would get overlooked without any context about what it is and how valuable it was. The Cost Calculator serves as both a reference tool and entry point into the Talent Marketplace. It helps customers understand the factors affecting the cost of hiring a remote engineer through Terminal. Based on input data from the users, the Cost Calculator suggests candidates in the Talent Marketplace who may be a good fit.
