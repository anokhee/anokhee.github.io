Product Design at Terminal

Problem Space

As an Employer of Record, Terminal enables businesses to outsource full-time software engineering talent without setting up a local entity. Through its convenient recruiting platform, the top 7% of candidates are vetted and onboarded for customers to hire.

In a jobseekers’ market like that of 2022, companies with the smoothest, fastest hiring process usually land the best hires. For this reason, customers churned when their times-to-fill with Terminal were longer than expected.

Discovery

Although an MVP technically existed, it was little more than a placeholder UI surfacing ATS data. I was encouraged to ignore it and design from first principles.

Discovery was grounded in user research, competitive analysis, and journey mapping. I mapped the recruiting journey from awareness to retention (Terminal typically entered at the Selection stage). I also did a deep dive into Terminal’s “Recruiter” persona, who I learned was usually not a recruiter at all. Many of Terminal’s customers were startups, so hiring might fall to a founder, manager, or another employee wearing multiple hats. To round this out, I researched other Employers of Record to understand industry standards.

Customers didn’t have a self-serve platform like candidates did. Most interactions with Terminal occurred over phone and email, so communication lag time could add up to delay hiring by days or longer.
